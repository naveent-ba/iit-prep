# JEE Preparation - Conceptual Framework Project

## Purpose
A set of conceptual guides that provide a mental framework for JEE preparation. These documents address the root cause of why students struggle with higher-level mathematics, physics, and chemistry: they memorize mechanics without understanding the underlying structure.

- Can solve problems at home that he fails in exam
- Issue is not knowledge but **access under time pressure**

### Subject Tendencies
- Strong in thinking subjects: Math, Physics
- Weaker in memory subjects: Chemistry (most scoring, biggest opportunity)
- Struggles with lengthy calculations/numericals even in strong subjects

## The Central Framework: Invariance

> **Mathematics is the study of invariants under transformation.**

This principle must remain central across ALL documents because:
- JEE (especially Advanced) filters for students who see structure, not those who memorize
- Pattern recognition under pressure requires a unifying mental model
- Each chapter must connect back to: "What changes? What remains invariant?"

## Document Usage
- Read **before practice** to anchor the right mindset
- Read **before exams** to reset mental state and reduce freeze
- **Standalone** - complements but doesn't replace coaching materials, NCERT, video tutorials

## Document Style Guidelines

### Structure (per chapter)
1. PAGE 1: "Why X Exists" - Mindset Reset (break misconceptions)
2. PAGE 2: The Core Idea - Anchor page with invariance framing
3. PAGE 3: What Changes vs What Remains Invariant (table)
4. Conceptual development pages
5. JEE Main vs JEE Advanced distinction
6. Skill-Based Examples (what is being tested)
7. Common Traps (exam killers)
8. Mental Checklist (before solving)
9. Forward Connections (links to other chapters)

### Tone
- Direct, no fluff
- "This is what's really happening" approach
- Formulas as consequences, not foundations
- Always ask: What is the invariant?

### Avoid
- Excessive formula lists (he has those elsewhere)
- Step-by-step procedural instructions
- Anything that encourages memorization over understanding
- **Content beyond JEE Main/Advanced syllabus** - do not add complexity with concepts or examples outside exam scope

### Chapter Philosophy
- Only create NEW chapters for genuinely different conceptual lenses
- Sub-topics belong INSIDE existing chapters, not as separate chapters
- "One subject, many lenses" - don't fragment the mental model
